Neshama

Community support when it matters most
When someone in the community passes away, everyone wants to help — but coordinating that help shouldn't be another burden. Neshama brings everything together in one place, so families can grieve while their community takes care of the rest.
1

A memorial appears

Obituaries are gathered automatically from local Jewish funeral homes. Families don't need to do anything — the listing is already there.

2

Someone wants to help

A friend finds the listing and sees clear options: bring food, send something meaningful, or help prepare the home.

3

Meals get organized

In minutes, a friend sets up a meal schedule — dates, dietary needs, drop-off details. No account needed. Just a simple form.

4

The link goes out

Share the page on WhatsApp, email, or text. Friends click and sign up for a day. No login. No password.

5

The community responds

The meal calendar fills in. Everyone sees what's covered and what's still needed. No duplicates, no gaps, no group-text chaos.

6

Beyond the meals

Tributes on the memorial page. A yahrzeit reminder a year later. The connection doesn't end when shiva does.
