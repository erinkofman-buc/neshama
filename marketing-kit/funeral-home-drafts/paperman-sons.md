Dear Paperman & Sons team,

My name is Erin Kofman. I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal, and I wanted to introduce myself.

Neshama features obituaries from Paperman & Sons — your listings appear alongside Toronto funeral homes so that community members across both cities can find everything in one search. For Montreal families especially, Paperman is often the first name they look for. Hundreds of people already use the site each week.

We're officially launching on March 24, and as we prepare, I wanted to make sure the funeral homes whose listings are at the heart of Neshama know about us — and have a voice in how we move forward.

**Why I'm reaching out:**

Every Paperman listing on Neshama links directly back to your original page. We're extending your reach to families who might not have found the listing otherwise — including people in Toronto looking for Montreal services. But I wanted you to know we exist, and I wanted to offer you a say in how your listings appear.

If you'd prefer a different format, want your logo included, or would rather provide a direct data feed instead of us pulling from the public site — I'd welcome that.

**What Neshama does beyond obituaries:**

- **Meal coordination for shiva** — families set up a page, volunteers sign up for specific days. Everyone sees what's covered and what's still needed, so nothing falls through the cracks.
- **"How Can I Help?" hub** — 120+ local food and gift vendors for people who want to send something meaningful.
- **Yahrzeit reminders** — annual email reminders based on the Hebrew calendar, sent before the date so families have time to prepare.
- **Condolence guestbook** — every memorial page includes a place for messages of comfort.

**Free. No ads. No accounts.** Neshama exists to serve the community — the same families you've been serving for generations.

I'd love for this to be a partnership. If there's a way Neshama can be useful to the families you serve — a link on your website, a mention during arrangements, a resource card at the chapel — I'd be grateful. And if anything about how we present your listings needs changing, just say the word.

Here's a quick walkthrough of how it all works: neshama.ca/demo

Happy to talk anytime.
