Dear Steeles Memorial Chapel team,

My name is Erin Kofman. I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal, and I wanted to introduce myself.

Neshama features obituaries from Steeles Memorial Chapel. We pull listings from your public website and display them alongside other local funeral homes so families and community members can find everything in one search. Hundreds of people use the site each week to find obituaries, coordinate shiva meals, and support families going through a loss.

**Why I'm reaching out:**

Every Steeles listing on Neshama links directly back to your original page — we drive visitors to you, not away. But I wanted you to know we exist, and I wanted to offer you a say in how your listings appear.

If you'd ever prefer we adjust the format, add your logo, or switch to a direct data feed instead of pulling from the public site — I'm completely open to that.

**What Neshama does beyond obituaries:**

- **Meal coordination for shiva** — families set up a page, volunteers sign up for specific days. Everyone sees what's covered and what's still needed, so nothing falls through the cracks.
- **"How Can I Help?" hub** — 120+ local food and gift vendors for people who want to send something meaningful.
- **Yahrzeit reminders** — annual email reminders based on the Hebrew calendar, sent before the date so families have time to prepare.
- **Condolence guestbook** — every memorial page includes a place for messages of comfort.

**Free. No ads. No accounts.** Neshama exists to serve the community — the same families you serve every day.

I'd love for this to be a partnership. If there's a way Neshama can be useful to your families — a link on your website, a mention during arrangements, a resource card in your office — I'd be grateful. And if there's anything you'd like changed about how we present your listings, just say the word.

Happy to meet in person or hop on a call anytime.

Warm regards,
