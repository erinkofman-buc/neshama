Dear Beth Zion team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Montreal and Toronto who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Paperman & Sons and Misaskim in Montreal, alongside Steeles Memorial and Benjamin's Park in Toronto, into one searchable feed. Every obituary links directly back to the funeral home's own page, so the primary source always stays intact.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status so the food that arrives respects the family's standards.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. It's a coordination tool the community can use — not affiliated with any movement or organization.

I know Beth Zion is in an interim chapter on the rabbinic side, so I wanted to send this to the office rather than make any assumptions. If it feels useful for congregants or your chesed network, I'd be glad to send a short blurb suitable for a bulletin or email — and you're welcome to pass it along to Rabbi Benarroch or your leadership in whatever way feels right.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for everything you do for Montreal's Jewish community.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
neshama.ca | contact@neshama.ca
