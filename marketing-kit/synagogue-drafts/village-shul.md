Dear Village Shul team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. Every listing links directly back to the funeral home's own website, so the primary source stays intact.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family or a close friend sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status. The goal is simple: when someone in the community passes, the people who want to help know exactly how — without a dozen overlapping group chats or duplicate deliveries.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. It's a coordination tool the community can use, not affiliated with any movement.

I think Village Shul congregants and your chesed network would find this useful, both when they experience a loss themselves and when they want to support someone who is grieving. If it feels valuable, I'd be glad to send a short blurb for your weekly email or bulletin.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for the warm community you've built downtown.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
