Dear Adath Israel team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. Every obituary links directly back to the funeral home's own page, so families and community members are always seeing the primary source.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. Families (or a friend acting on their behalf) set up a page, and volunteers sign up for specific nights so meals don't overlap and nothing falls through the cracks. The caterer directory can be filtered for kosher options when that matters to the family.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. We built it because when a family is sitting shiva, everyone wants to help — they just need a respectful way to coordinate that help.

I think this could be a meaningful resource to share with Adath Israel congregants, your chesed committee, or anyone who fields the "what can I do?" calls when there's a loss in the community.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

If this feels useful, I'd be glad to send a short blurb for your newsletter or bulletin. Thank you for the care you provide to Toronto's Jewish community.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
