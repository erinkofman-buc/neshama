Dear Kehillat Shaarei Torah team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. Every listing links directly back to the funeral home's own page, so families and community members are always seeing the primary source.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status so the food that arrives respects the family's standards.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. It's a coordination tool the community can use — not affiliated with any movement or organization.

I think Kehillat Shaarei Torah congregants and your chesed network would find this useful, both when they experience a loss themselves and when they want to show up for someone else. If it feels valuable, I'd be glad to send a short blurb for your weekly email or bulletin.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for the work you do for the kehillah.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
neshama.ca | contact@neshama.ca
