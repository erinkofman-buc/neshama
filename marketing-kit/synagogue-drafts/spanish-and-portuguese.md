# Synagogue Outreach Draft — Spanish & Portuguese Synagogue (Montreal)

**To:** office@thespanish.org
**From:** contact@neshama.ca
**Subject:** A free resource for Spanish & Portuguese families during bereavement

---

Dear Spanish & Portuguese team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Montreal and Toronto who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from the major Jewish funeral homes serving Montreal and Toronto — Paperman & Sons and Misaskim on the Montreal side, alongside Steeles Memorial and Benjamin's Park in Toronto — into one searchable feed. Every obituary links directly back to the funeral home's own page, so the primary source always stays intact.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status. No group chat chaos, no overlapping deliveries.

I want to be honest about one thing: Neshama currently aggregates from primarily Ashkenazi funeral homes, and we know the Spanish & Portuguese community has its own distinct minhag and traditions around aveilut. We'd love any guidance on how Neshama could be more useful or more respectful of the kahal's customs over time — that kind of feedback would mean a lot.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

If this feels valuable to share with congregants or your chesed network, I'd be glad to send a short blurb for your bulletin.

Thank you for everything you do.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
neshama.ca | contact@neshama.ca

---

**Why this framing:** Sephardic congregation with a distinct minhag — explicitly acknowledged the Ashkenazi-leaning data sources, named the distinct minhag and aveilut customs respectfully, and asked for guidance rather than pretending we already serve the kahal perfectly. Used "kahal" once to honour the community's self-reference.
