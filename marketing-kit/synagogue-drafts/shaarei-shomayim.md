Dear Shaarei Shomayim team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. Every listing links directly back to the funeral home's own website, so nothing drifts from the primary source.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family or a friend sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status so the food that arrives respects the family's standards. No group chat chaos, no overlap, no awkward "did anyone bring dinner Tuesday?" texts.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. We built it because when someone in the community passes, everyone wants to help — they just need a way to coordinate that help with dignity.

I think this could be a meaningful resource to share with congregants and your chesed committee. If it feels useful, I'd be glad to send a short blurb suitable for a newsletter or bulletin.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for the work you do for Toronto's Jewish community.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
neshama.ca | contact@neshama.ca
