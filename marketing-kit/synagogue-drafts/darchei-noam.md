Hi Claire,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss, and I'm hoping it might be something Darchei Noam would consider sharing with congregants.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool: a family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, and volunteers sign up for specific nights so meals don't overlap and nothing falls through the cracks.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. We built it because when someone in the community passes, everyone wants to help — they just need a respectful, simple way to coordinate that help.

I thought of Darchei Noam because the Reconstructionist tradition of community-led care and chesed feels so aligned with what we're trying to support. This is the kind of thing a caring committee or a congregant supporting a friend could turn to without having to figure out a dozen logistics from scratch.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

If it feels useful, I'd be happy to send a short blurb suitable for your newsletter or bulletin — and of course pass anything along to Rabbi Grimberg if and when feels right on your end.

Thank you for everything you do.

Warmly,
