Dear BAYT team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. Every listing links directly back to the funeral home's own website, so the primary source stays intact and nothing is rewritten or pulled out of context.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status so the food that arrives respects the family's standards. No group chat chaos, no overlap, no awkward "did anyone bring dinner Tuesday?" texts.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. It's a coordination tool the community can use — not affiliated with any movement or organization.

Given the size of the BAYT kehillah and the chesed work your community already does so well, I think this could genuinely save volunteers and chesed coordinators a lot of back-and-forth. If it feels valuable, I'd be glad to send a short blurb for your weekly email, bulletin, or chesed committee distribution list.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for the work you do for Toronto's Jewish community.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
neshama.ca | contact@neshama.ca
