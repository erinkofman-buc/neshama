Dear Beth Sholom team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from the major Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool: a family sets up a page, friends and community members sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory makes it easy to find local food when someone wants to send a meal but doesn't know where to start.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. We see it as a small extension of the chesed work that bereavement and caring committees already do — one link to share when someone in the community passes, so nothing falls through the cracks.

I think this could be genuinely useful for Beth Sholom families, both when they experience a loss and when they want to support someone who is grieving.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

If this feels valuable, I'd be glad to send a short write-up suitable for your newsletter, bulletin, or bereavement resources page. Thank you for everything you do for the Toronto Jewish community.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
