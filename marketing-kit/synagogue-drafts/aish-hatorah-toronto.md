Dear Rabbi Diamond,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss, and I wanted to share it with you.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Toronto and Montreal Jewish funeral homes — Steeles Memorial, Benjamin's Park, Paperman & Sons, and Misaskim — into one searchable feed. Every listing links directly back to the funeral home's own website, so nothing is rewritten or pulled out of context from the primary source.

It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool. A family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, volunteers sign up for specific nights, and the caterer directory can be filtered by kosher status. No group chat chaos, no overlap, and the food that arrives respects the family's standards.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. It's not affiliated with any movement or organization — just a coordination tool the community can use.

I think this could be useful for the Aish Toronto community, particularly for chesed coordination when someone in the kehillah experiences a loss. If it feels valuable, I'd be glad to send a short blurb suitable for an email or WhatsApp share.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for the work you do.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
