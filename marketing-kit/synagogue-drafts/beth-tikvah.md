Dear Beth Tikvah team,

My name is Erin Kofman. Together with my co-founder Jordana Mednick, I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Montreal and Toronto who are navigating a loss.

Neshama brings together obituary listings from Paperman & Sons and Misaskim in Montreal, alongside Steeles Memorial and Benjamin's Park in Toronto, into one searchable feed. It also includes a shiva meal coordination tool: a family (or a friend on their behalf) sets up a page, and volunteers sign up for specific nights so meals don't overlap and nothing falls through the cracks.

Everything is free for families. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls. We see it as a small extension of the chesed work that bereavement and caring committees already do — one link to share when someone in the community passes.

I know Beth Tikvah is in an interim chapter on the spiritual leadership side with Cantor Topas, and I wanted to send this directly to the office rather than make any assumptions about who's best to receive it. If it feels useful, I'd be glad to send a short write-up for your newsletter or bulletin — and you're welcome to pass it along to Cantor Topas, your bereavement committee, or lay leadership in whatever way feels right on your end.

You can see a quick walkthrough of how it all works here: neshama.ca/demo

Thank you for everything you do for Montreal's West Island Jewish community.

Warmly,
Erin Kofman & Jordana Mednick
neshama.ca | contact@neshama.ca
