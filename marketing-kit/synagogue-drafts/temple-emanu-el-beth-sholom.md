Dear Temple Emanu-El-Beth Sholom team,

My name is Erin Kofman. I built Neshama (neshama.ca) — a free resource for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are navigating a loss.

Neshama pulls obituaries from Paperman & Sons and Misaskim into one searchable feed, alongside Toronto sources. It also offers a meal coordination tool for shiva — families or friends can set up a page, and volunteers sign up for specific nights so nothing overlaps.

The site includes a directory of 120+ local food and gift vendors, and a shiva guide for anyone who wants to understand the traditions.

Everything is free. No ads on obituary pages. No paywalls.

If this is something you'd like to share with congregants — in a newsletter, bulletin, or bereavement resources page — I'd be happy to provide a short write-up.

Thank you for all you do for Montreal's Jewish community.
