## Wednesday Apr 2 — Holidays & Grief (Passover)

**Post time:** 10 AM
**File:** `instagram-posts/apr02-holidays-grief.png`
**Pillar:** Community Connection

**Caption:**

The seder table is set. But someone's chair is empty.

Holidays can be the hardest days for those carrying grief. Everyone around you is celebrating, and that joy can make the absence feel even louder. The recipe nobody else makes quite the same way. The voice missing from the songs. The seat that stays open.

If this Pesach feels heavier than usual — you're not alone. Your community sees you. Your feelings don't need to be tucked away to make room for the holiday.

Hold both things: the gratitude for freedom and the ache of missing someone. They can sit at the table together.

Tag someone who might need to hear this today.

## Friday Apr 4 — Meal Coordination Feature

**Post time:** 10 AM
**File:** `instagram-posts/apr04-meal-coordination.png`
**Pillar:** Product Awareness

**Caption:**

Three families bring lasagna. Nobody brings breakfast. The vegetarian goes hungry. Sound familiar?

Coordinating meals during shiva shouldn't add stress to an already difficult time. That's why we built Neshama's meal coordination tool.

Here's how it works:
1. An organizer creates a meal schedule for the mourning family
2. They share the link with friends, neighbours, and community
3. Volunteers sign up for specific meals — no duplicates, no gaps
4. The family gets nourished. No group texts. No guessing.

Because feeding a mourning family is a beautiful act of chesed. It just works better when it's organized.

Try it at neshama.ca

**Pillar:** Founder Story / Behind the Scenes

**Caption:**

We launched Neshama on March 24. One week ago. Here's what we've learned so far — honestly.

People check. Every single morning, community members visit neshama.ca to see who passed and whether there's someone they need to show up for. That daily ritual we hoped for? It's happening.

Meal coordination matters. It's the feature people share most. When someone is sitting shiva, the community wants to help — they just need an easy way to do it without the chaos of group texts.

This work is personal. Every message we receive — from someone who found out about a passing through Neshama, or coordinated meals for a family they love — reminds us why Jordana and I built this.

We're one week in. We have so much more planned. But this week reminded us that the simplest version of showing up — just being there — is enough.

Thank you for being part of this.
