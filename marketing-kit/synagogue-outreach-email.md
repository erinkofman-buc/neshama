## Email Body

Dear {{rabbi_name}},

My name is Erin Kofman. I built a resource for Jewish families in {{city}} called Neshama (neshama.ca), and I wanted to share it with you.

When someone in our community passes away, the people who want to help always have the same questions. Where's the obituary? Has anyone organized meals? What day should I bring food? Who can I call?

Neshama puts all of that in one place.

**What it does:**

1. **Obituary feed** — Pulls directly from the public funeral home websites (Steeles, Benjamin's, Paperman & Sons) so families don't have to check three different sites.

2. **Meal coordination** — A family sets up a shiva page. Volunteers sign up for specific days. Everyone sees what's covered and what's still needed. No more group chat chaos.

3. **"How Can I Help?" hub** — 120+ local food partners and gift vendors. Browse by category, city, kosher status. Find what you need in a minute.

4. **Yahrzeit reminders** — Annual email reminders based on the Hebrew calendar. Arrives before the date so there's time to prepare.

5. **Condolence guestbook** — Each memorial page has a place for community members to leave messages for the family.

**Free. No accounts. No ads.** Neshama exists to serve the community.

**Why I'm writing:**

I think families at {{synagogue_name}} would find this useful — both when they experience a loss and when they want to support someone who is grieving. It's the kind of resource that's most helpful when people know about it before they need it.

If you think it's valuable, any of these would make a real difference:

- Share the link (neshama.ca) in your weekly email or newsletter
- Mention it to your chesed or bikur cholim committee
- Add it to your list of community resources
- Use the blurb below — it's ready to copy-paste

Happy to answer any questions. I built this because I saw how much families struggle to coordinate during a loss, and I want it to be as useful as possible.

Warm regards,
Erin Kofman
neshama.ca
contact@neshama.ca

## Follow-Up Protocol

- **Day 1:** Send personalized email
- **Day 7:** If no response: "Hi {{rabbi_name}} — following up on my note about Neshama. Happy to answer any questions or send additional info for your congregation."
- **Day 14:** If still no response: "Would it help if I sent a ready-made blurb for your newsletter? I have one prepared."
- **Day 21+:** No response after two follow-ups — mark as contacted and move on.

## Notes for Sending

- Send from contact@neshama.ca via SendGrid
- One at a time — never bulk
- If you know the rabbi's name, use it. Otherwise "Dear Rabbi" or "Dear Synagogue Office"
- For Montreal: mention Paperman & Sons specifically — it carries weight
- Tone check: would this feel respectful if it arrived the week a congregant passed away? If yes, send.
