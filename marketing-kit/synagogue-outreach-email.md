## Email Body

Dear {{rabbi_name}},

My name is Erin Kofman. I am writing to share a resource I built for Jewish families in {{city}} called Neshama (neshama.ca).

When someone in our community passes away, the people who want to help often struggle with the same questions: Where do I find the obituary? Has anyone organized meals for the family? What day should I bring food? Who can I call to send something?

Neshama brings the answers to these questions into one place.

**What Neshama provides:**

1. **Obituary feed** — Listings from Steeles Memorial Chapel, Benjamin's Park Memorial Chapel, and Paperman & Sons, updated regularly and searchable. Families and community members no longer need to check multiple funeral home websites.

2. **Meal coordination** — When a family is sitting shiva, a coordination page lets community members sign up for specific days. Everyone can see what is covered and what is still needed. It replaces the chain of phone calls and group messages that often leave gaps or overlaps.

3. **"How Can I Help?" hub** — Over 110 local food partners and gift vendors who provide meals, catering, and sympathy gifts for families in mourning. Supporters can browse by category and city to find what they need.

4. **Yahrzeit reminders** — Community members can set up annual email reminders for a loved one's yahrzeit, based on the Hebrew calendar. The reminder arrives before the date so they have time to prepare.

5. **Digital condolence guestbook** — Each memorial page includes a place for community members to leave messages of comfort for the family.

**Everything is provided at no cost.** No accounts required. No advertisements. Neshama exists to serve the community.

**Why I am writing to you:**

I believe the families at {{synagogue_name}} would find Neshama helpful — both when they experience a loss themselves and when they want to support someone else who is grieving. It is the kind of resource that is most useful when people know about it before they need it.

If you think it would be valuable, I would be grateful if you would consider any of the following:

- Sharing the link (neshama.ca) in your weekly email or newsletter
- Mentioning it to your chesed or bikur cholim committee
- Including it in your list of community resources
- Sharing the newsletter blurb below

I welcome any feedback or questions. I built Neshama because I saw how much families struggle to coordinate support during a loss, and I want it to be as useful as possible for communities like yours.

With warm regards,
Erin Kofman
neshama.ca
contact@neshama.ca

## Follow-Up Protocol

- **Day 1:** Send personalized email
- **Day 7:** If no response: "Dear {{rabbi_name}}, I wanted to follow up on my note about Neshama. I am happy to answer any questions or provide additional information for your congregation."
- **Day 14:** If still no response, send with a specific offer: "Would it be helpful if I provided a short blurb ready for your newsletter? I have one prepared and can send it along."
- **Day 21+:** If no response after two follow-ups, mark as contacted and move on. Do not send further emails.

---

## Notes for Sending

- Send from contact@neshama.ca via SendGrid
- One email at a time — never bulk
- If you know the rabbi's name, use it. If not, "Dear Rabbi" or "Dear Synagogue Office" is fine.
- For Montreal synagogues, mention Paperman & Sons specifically — it carries weight
- Tone check before sending: would this feel respectful if it arrived the week a congregant passed away? If yes, send it.
