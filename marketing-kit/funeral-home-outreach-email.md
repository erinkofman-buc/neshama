# Funeral Home Outreach Email Template
Updated: March 13, 2026

Different pitch from vendors/synagogues. These are our data sources. The goal is transparency + partnership, not selling anything.

**Variables:**
- `{{funeral_home}}` — Full name
- `{{city}}` — Toronto or Montreal
- `{{contact_email}}` — Their email (verify before sending)

---

## Subject Line Options

1. Neshama.ca — your obituaries are reaching more families
2. A community resource built on top of {{funeral_home}}'s listings
3. Neshama.ca + {{funeral_home}} — supporting families together

---

## Email Body

Hi there,

My name is Erin Kofman. I built a free community resource called Neshama (neshama.ca) for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal.

I wanted to reach out because Neshama features obituaries from {{funeral_home}}. We pull listings from your public website and display them alongside other local funeral homes so families and community members can find everything in one place.

**Why this matters:** When someone passes away, the people who want to pay their respects or help the family have to check multiple funeral home websites. Neshama solves that. One search, all the local listings.

**What Neshama does beyond obituaries:**

- **Meal coordination for shiva** — families set up a page, volunteers sign up for specific days.
- **"How Can I Help?" hub** — 120+ local food and gift vendors for people who want to send something meaningful.
- **Yahrzeit reminders** — annual email reminders based on the Hebrew calendar.
- **Condolence guestbook** — every memorial page has a place for messages of comfort.

**Free. No ads. No accounts.** Neshama exists to serve the community.

**What I'd like you to know:**

- Every {{funeral_home}} listing on Neshama links back to your original page. We drive traffic to you, not away.
- We don't modify obituary content. What families see on Neshama is what you published.
- If you'd ever like us to adjust how your listings appear — or if you'd prefer to provide a direct data feed instead of us scraping the public site — I'm very open to that.

I'd love for this to be a partnership. If there's a way Neshama can be useful to the families you serve — a link on your site, a mention during arrangements, a resource card in your office — I'd be grateful.

Happy to meet or hop on a call anytime.

Warm regards,
Erin Kofman
neshama.ca
contact@neshama.ca

## Key Messaging Notes

- **Never apologize for scraping.** The data is public. Frame it as extending their reach.
- **Always offer the data feed alternative.** Shows good faith.
- **Lead with value to them:** we send traffic back, we help their families coordinate.
- **Partnership ask is soft:** link on their site, mention to families, flyer in office.
- **No upsell.** This is not a vendor relationship. This is a source relationship.
- **Follow up once at Day 7.** If no response, wait. These are long-term relationships.
