# PART 1: FACEBOOK PAGE SETUP

## Step-by-Step Page Creation

1. **Go to** facebook.com/pages/create (must be logged into a personal account)
2. **Page name:** Neshama
3. **Category:** Select **"Community Organization"**
   - Why not "Nonprofit": Neshama is a for-profit social enterprise. Facebook may eventually require nonprofit verification documents you don't have.
   - Why not "Website" or "Internet Company": Those categories look like tech startups, not community resources.
   - "Community Organization" signals trust, local roots, and public service — exactly the right framing for grief support.
   - Secondary category: add **"Community Service"** as well (Facebook allows up to 3).
4. **Bio/intro line (155 char limit):** "A free resource for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal. Obituaries, shiva meal coordination, and local vendors."
5. Click **Create Page**

### Rules of Engagement for Facebook Groups

**Before posting:**
- Join the group and wait at least 24-48 hours
- Read the pinned post / group rules. Most groups prohibit:
  - Direct advertising or promotional posts
  - Links without context
  - Repeat posting of the same content
- Many groups DO allow:
  - Community resource sharing (which is what Neshama is)
  - Personal stories that include a resource
  - Responding to relevant threads (e.g., "does anyone know where to find..." or "we need help coordinating meals for...")
- Like/comment on a few other posts first so you're a visible community member, not a drive-by poster

**Best approach:** Don't post cold. Wait for a natural opening — someone asking about shiva logistics, funeral information, or meal coordination. Reply with the resource. If posting proactively, use Version A or B from Part 3 below.
