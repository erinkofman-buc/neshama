Neshama is free.
And it always will be.

Every family deserves to know when someone in their community has passed away. That is why Neshama will never put obituaries behind a paywall, charge for meal coordination, or gate access to our directory.

But keeping this service running takes real resources. And right now, it is sustained by the people who care about it most.

In Jewish tradition, the number 18 represents chai — life. When we give in multiples of 18, we extend a blessing: may there be life, may there be goodness.

Your support is not a transaction. It is an act of communal care — a way of saying that remembering our loved ones matters, and that this work is worth sustaining.

Giving back

10% of all sustainer revenue is directed to local Jewish community organizations in Toronto and Montreal. Your support strengthens Neshama and the broader community.

Sustaining Neshama is a voluntary contribution to support the platform, not a charitable donation. Contributions are not tax-deductible. Neshama is not a registered charity and cannot issue tax receipts.
