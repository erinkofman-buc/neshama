About Neshama

Honoring every soul in our community

Our Mission

In Jewish tradition, honoring the deceased (kavod hamet) and comforting mourners (nichum aveilim) are among the highest mitzvot. Neshama exists to make these sacred obligations easier for our community.

We aggregate obituary listings from Jewish funeral homes in Toronto and Montreal into one respectful feed, so you never miss an opportunity to pay your respects, attend a funeral, or comfort a grieving family.

What Does 'Neshama' Mean?

Neshama (נשמה) is the Hebrew word for 'soul.' In Jewish mystical thought, the neshama is the highest level of the soul — the divine spark that connects each person to the Creator. It is eternal, pure, and sacred.

We chose this name because every soul deserves to be remembered. Every life, no matter how long or short, leaves an imprint on our community. Neshama ensures that no one is forgotten.

What We Do

Aggregate

We collect obituary listings from Jewish funeral homes across Canada, currently including Steeles Memorial Chapel and Benjamin's Park Memorial Chapel in Toronto, and Paperman & Sons in Montreal.

Inform

We provide daily email digests, searchable archives, and instant access to funeral and shiva details so you can be there when it matters.

Remember

We offer memorial pages with tributes and a space for the community to share memories and honor those we've lost.

Our Values

  • Kavod (כבוד) — Dignity
    We treat every listing with the utmost respect and sensitivity.
  • Chesed (חסד) — Kindness
    Our service is free because supporting the community shouldn't have a price.
  • Emet (אמת) — Truth
    We present accurate information sourced directly from funeral homes.
  • Tzniut (צניעות) — Modesty
    We operate with restraint — no ads, no tracking, no exploitation.

Serving the Community Since 2026

Neshama is an independent community service built to strengthen the bonds between members of the Jewish community. When one family mourns, we all stand with them. Our goal is simple: ensure that no passing goes unnoticed and that every mourner feels the embrace of their community.
