Shiva Essentials

Things the community may need when preparing for shiva — gathered in one place so you can focus on what matters most.

Memorial & Prayer

The items at the heart of the shiva home — the candle that burns for seven days, and the prayer books that bring comfort to those who gather.

7-Day Memorial Candle

The ner neshama burns throughout the shiva period. A tall, long-lasting yahrzeit candle in a glass jar is the traditional choice.

Browse memorial candles →

Siddur (Prayer Book)

Visitors will need siddurim for minyan services. Having several on hand means no one has to share or go without during prayers.

Browse prayer books →

Tehillim (Book of Psalms)

Many mourners find solace in reading Psalms. A dedicated Tehillim with English translation is especially meaningful.

Browse Tehillim →

The Shiva Home

Practical items that help transform a home for the week of mourning.

Low Shiva Chairs

Mourners traditionally sit on low chairs or stools during the shiva period. Folding step stools or low camping chairs can work well.

Browse low chairs →

Folding Chairs for Guests

You may need extra seating for the many visitors who come to pay their respects, especially during evening services.

Browse folding chairs →

Mirrors Covering

It is customary to cover mirrors in the shiva home. Simple fabric or dark sheets work well for this purpose.

Browse fabric covers →

Food & Serving

Whether you are ordering from a caterer or the community is bringing meals, these items help keep things organized.

Disposable Serving Platters

Aluminum trays and platters make it easy to serve, reheat, and clean up without burdening the family with dishes.

Browse serving platters →

Paper Goods & Cutlery

Plates, cups, napkins, and utensils. Choose sturdy ones — flimsy plates with hot food are no one's friend.

Browse paper goods →

Coffee & Tea Supplies

A well-stocked coffee and tea station is one of the most appreciated things in a shiva home. Don't forget sugar, milk, and cups.

Browse coffee & tea →
Tip: If the family keeps kosher, consider using a local kosher caterer for the main meals. This takes the guesswork out of kashrut and lets volunteers focus on support rather than logistics.

Sympathy Cards

A handwritten note of condolence means more than most people realize. Simple, heartfelt cards are always appropriate.

Books on Grief & Mourning

Books that address Jewish mourning traditions or offer comfort through grief can be a lasting and meaningful gift.
