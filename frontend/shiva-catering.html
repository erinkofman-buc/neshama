Frequently asked questions

Can I order shiva food for same-day delivery?

Yes. Many vendors in our directory offer same-day or next-day delivery within their service zones. Filter by Delivers and your delivery area to see who can serve the family today. Call the vendor directly to confirm same-day capacity, since this varies by demand.

What is the difference between kosher and kosher-style catering?

Kosher means the vendor is certified by a recognized kashrut authority — in Toronto, that is COR (Council of Orthodox Rabbis); in Montreal, MK (Vaad Hair). Kosher-style means traditional Jewish food (deli, bagels, brisket) prepared in a kitchen that is not certified. If the family observes kashrut, only certified kosher vendors are appropriate. If they do not, kosher-style is welcome.

How much food should I order for a shiva?

A common rule is one platter per 8 to 10 visitors during a single visiting block, plus a meal for the immediate family. For shiva that runs several days, coordinate with other friends and family so meals are spread across the week, not all delivered the first afternoon. Most caterers can recommend portions based on expected visitor count. More on what to bring →

Can I coordinate with other friends sending meals?

Yes. Neshama offers a free meal coordination tool for the family or a designated organizer. It shows what is being brought on each day so contributions are spread out and nothing duplicates. Find the meal page on the family's listing or set one up if there is not one yet.

Do I need to know the family's dietary restrictions?

If the family observes kashrut, only certified kosher food is appropriate. Beyond that, common considerations include nut allergies, vegetarian or vegan needs, and gluten-free preferences. If you are not sure, ask the family or the meal coordinator. When in doubt, choose neutral options that work for most diets.
