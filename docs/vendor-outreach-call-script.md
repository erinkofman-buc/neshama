# Vendor Outreach — Copyable Call Script

> Grief-reduced-friction script for cold-calling caterers, restaurants, and gift platter vendors to invite them onto the Neshama directory. Tone: warm, community-first, never salesy. Read it once, then make it your own.
>
> When to use: when emails get no response after 7-10 days, OR when a vendor seems likely to convert with a personal touch (already serves shiva orders, has a strong community presence). Per `REVENUE-OPS.md`: don't pitch featured listings until vendor has 30+ clicks/month.

---

## 15-second opener

> "Hi, this is [your name] calling from Neshama. We're a free community resource for Jewish families in Toronto and Montreal who are organizing a shiva. We've been listing local caterers and food vendors who serve the community, and I wanted to make sure you knew you're [already on the directory / a great fit for the directory]. Do you have a quick minute?"

**Pacing notes:**
- Lead with **who you are + what Neshama is**, not what you're asking for
- "Free community resource" up front — defuses any "is this a sales call?" tension
- "Already on the directory" if they exist in the seed data; "a great fit" if you're inviting them new
- Always ask permission to continue ("a quick minute?") — respect their time

---

## The ask

> "I just wanted to let you know your listing is live at [neshama.ca/shiva-catering], and to ask if there's anything you'd want updated — your photo, your delivery zones, any details about how families can order from you. It takes maybe two minutes, and there's no cost. We just want the listing to feel right for you."

**Pacing notes:**
- "No cost" only after explaining the value — don't lead with "free" (sounds like there's a catch)
- "Want the listing to feel right for you" — frames the call as service to them, not extraction from them
- Send them the direct URL: `https://neshama.ca/directory/<their-slug>`

---

## Objection handlers

### "How much does this cost?"

> "The basic listing is permanently free. We don't charge families and we don't gate any features. If down the road you want a featured placement at the top of the directory — that runs $49 a month and includes monthly analytics on how many people viewed and clicked through to your business. But that's something we'd only suggest after you've been listed a while and we have real traffic data to show you."

### "Is this only for kosher caterers?"

> "Not at all. Our directory includes kosher-certified vendors (COR in Toronto, MK in Montreal) and non-kosher options. Many families don't keep strictly kosher, and many shivas welcome any thoughtful contribution. We just label kosher status clearly so families can filter to what they need."

### "I don't deliver to [neighbourhood]"

> "That's fine — we have a delivery zone filter on the page so families can find vendors who serve their specific area. We just need to know your service area accurately. What zones do you currently deliver to?"

### "I already have a website / I'm on Google"

> "Of course. We're not trying to replace your website — we're a curated directory that families search specifically when they need shiva food. Most of our visitors are coordinators (adult children, friends, synagogue volunteers) who don't know which caterers in the community do shiva trays well. We help them find you."

### "Who's behind this?"

> "Neshama was founded by Erin Kofman, a Toronto pharmacist, and Jordana [last name], who organizes shiva meals for families across the GTA. It's not a startup — it's a community resource. Built by us, free for families, no investors, no ads."

### "I'd rather not be listed"

> "Totally understand. I'll remove your listing today. If you change your mind, the application is at neshama.ca/shiva/caterers/apply. Thanks for your time."

**(Then actually remove them. No follow-up. Respect the no.)**

## Featured upsell language (for later follow-up, NOT first call)

> "Hey [name], following up because your listing has been getting strong traffic — over [X] families clicked through to your site this month. We have a featured placement option at $49 a month that puts you at the top of the search results and gives you monthly analytics on views and clicks. One catered shiva order usually covers the cost several times over. Want me to send you the details?"

**When to use:**
- Vendor has 30+ clicks in a month (per REVENUE-OPS)
- Vendor has been listed at least 60 days
- You have data to show, not promises

**When NOT to use:**
- First call
- Vendor with < 30 clicks (would be embarrassing, not persuasive)
- Vendor whose listing hasn't been audited recently (don't pitch a featured spot if their info is wrong)

---

## What NOT to say

- ❌ "Our partners" (too commercial)
- ❌ "Premium" (sounds like a paywall)
- ❌ "We can drive customers to you" (overpromising)
- ❌ "Best in Toronto" (no superlatives)
- ❌ "We monetize the platform via..." (don't lecture about your business model)
- ❌ Anything that sounds like a sales pitch

## What to say instead

- ✅ "Listed in the community directory"
- ✅ "Featured placement" (when discussing $49/mo)
- ✅ "Families search specifically for shiva food"
- ✅ "We want to make sure your details are right"
- ✅ "The directory is free for families, always"

---

## After the call

1. **Update `data-quality-backlog.md` immediately** if they corrected any info (wrong website, wrong delivery area, etc.)
2. **Log the call** — date, who you spoke to, outcome (interested / not interested / asked for info)
3. **If they asked for follow-up info**, send within 24 hours via email
4. **If they said yes to featured listing** ($49/mo), DON'T process payment on the call. Send a follow-up email with the option, let them act on their own time. Reduces buyer's remorse and keeps the relationship community-feeling, not salesy.
