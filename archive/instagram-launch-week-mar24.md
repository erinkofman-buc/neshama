## POST 3 — Wednesday March 26 @ 10:00 AM
**Type:** Carousel (7 slides)
**Pillar:** Jewish Tradition & Education
**File:** `instagram-posts/carousel-shiva-guest/slide_1.png` through `slide_7.png`

**Image Description:**
- **Slide 1:** Cream background with terracotta text: "5 Things Every Shiva Guest Should Know" — Cormorant Garamond heading. Small candle icon. Subtext: "Save this for when you need it."
- **Slide 2:** "1. You don't have to say anything." Body text: "In Jewish tradition, visitors follow the mourner's lead. If they want to talk, listen. If they want silence, sit with them. Your presence is the gift."
- **Slide 3:** "2. Bring food — but coordinate first." Body text: "Three families bringing lasagna while nobody brings breakfast helps no one. Ask the organizer what's needed. Better yet, sign up through a meal coordination system."
- **Slide 4:** "3. Keep your visit short." Body text: "15-30 minutes is perfectly appropriate. A shiva house sees dozens of visitors — the family's energy is limited. Quality over quantity."
- **Slide 5:** "4. Don't say 'they're in a better place.'" Body text: "Skip the silver linings. Instead try: 'I'm so sorry.' 'I loved your mom's laugh.' 'Tell me about them.' Or simply: nothing at all."
- **Slide 6:** "5. Follow up after the week is over." Body text: "The hardest part often comes after shiva ends and everyone goes home. A text at shloshim (30 days), the yahrzeit, or just a random Tuesday means the world."
- **Slide 7:** Terracotta background, cream text: "Showing up matters more than showing up perfectly. neshama.ca" with candle icon.

**Caption:**
```
Most people want to do the right thing at a shiva. They're just not sure what that is.

Here are 5 things every shiva guest should know — whether it's your first shiva or your fiftieth.

The short version: show up, bring food (coordinated), listen more than you talk, and follow up after the week ends.

Save this. Share it with someone who might need it. Because knowing what to do makes it so much easier to actually do it.

#Neshama #ShivaGuide #JewishTraditions #NichumAveilim #ShivaEtiquette #JewishCommunity
```

**Scheduling:** 10:00 AM ET, Wednesday March 26

## How to Schedule Each Post

1. Open **Meta Business Suite** (business.facebook.com)
2. Click **Create post** → select Instagram
3. Upload the image(s) listed above
4. Paste the caption into the **Text** box
5. Click the arrow next to **Publish** → **Schedule** → set the date/time listed
6. Click **Schedule**

All image files should be saved to: `~/Desktop/Neshama/instagram-posts/`
